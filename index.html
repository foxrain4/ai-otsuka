Ai Otsuka – J-Pop Icon

Early Life and Musical Beginnings

Born on September 9, 1982, in Osaka, Japan, Ai Otsuka showed musical promise from a young age. She began playing piano at four years old and later graduated from Osaka University of Arts Junior College. Her passion for songwriting started early and led her to a deal with Avex Trax.

Breakthrough Success

Ai made her debut in 2003 with the single Momo no Hanabira, but her breakout hit was Sakuranbo. The song stayed on Japan’s Oricon Weekly Chart for over 100 weeks and became one of the most iconic J-pop singles of the 2000s.

Visual and Artistic Work

In 2024, Ai celebrated her 20th anniversary with AIO ART, a solo art exhibition held at Spiral Garden in Tokyo. She displayed oil paintings inspired by her lyrics and released a special piano instrumental album titled graine.

Legacy and Impact

Ai Otsuka remains a beloved figure in Japanese music, known for blending playful melodies with emotional storytelling. Her songs continue to resonate across generations, and her annual Love is Born concerts celebrate her enduring connection with fans.
