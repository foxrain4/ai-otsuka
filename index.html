Ai Otsuka

J-Pop Icon and Multidisciplinary Artist

Early Life & Musical Breakthrough

Born on September 9, 1982, in Osaka, Japan, Ai Otsuka began playing piano at the age of four. She composed her first song at 15 and pursued a degree in nursery education. Her major-label debut came in 2003 with the single "Momo no Hanabira," followed by the hit "Sakuranbo," which remained on the Oricon charts for over 100 weeks. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Discography Highlights

  • Love Punch (2004) – Debut album featuring "Sakuranbo"
  • Love Jam (2004) – Included hits like "Happy Days"
  • Love Cook (2005) – Featured "Smily/Biidama"
  • Love Piece (2007) – Notable for "Frienger"
  • Love Fantastic (2014) – Marked her return after a hiatus

Beyond Music

Otsuka is also an accomplished illustrator and author. She has published picture books and, in 2020, made her literary debut with the short story "Akecha Ikenai Ndayo" in the magazine Shosetsu Gendai. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

20th Anniversary & AIO ART

In July 2024, to commemorate her 20th anniversary, Otsuka held her first solo art exhibition, "AIO ART," at Tokyo's Spiral Garden. The exhibition featured oil paintings inspired by her songs and a limited-edition piano instrumental mini-album titled "graine." :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Legacy

With over 7 million records sold in Japan, Ai Otsuka continues to influence the J-pop scene through her music, art, and storytelling. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
